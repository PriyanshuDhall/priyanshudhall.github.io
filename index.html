About

Where language models meet real answers

Most people learn machine learning as theory. Priyanshu learned it by shipping something that had to actually work: a chatbot that reads a PDF and refuses to make things up. That one constraint — ground every answer in retrieved, verifiable context — is what pulled him from conventional software training into generative AI, and it's still the standard he holds every project to.

He's comfortable across the whole stack a GenAI product needs: chunking and embedding documents, storing them in a vector database, orchestrating retrieval with LangChain, and wiring the result into an interface a non-technical user could actually open and trust. An AWS Cloud Practitioner certification and a frontend training background round him out — he can reason about the infrastructure underneath a model and the interface in front of it, not just the model in the middle.

What he's looking for now is a team building AI products for real users, where "it works in the demo" isn't good enough — and where a fast learner who ships is worth more than a long resume.
